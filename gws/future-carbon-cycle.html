Historic \(\text{CO}_2\) Levels

We have two kinds of direct measurements of past \(\text{CO}_2\) levels in the atmosphere. One comes from ancient bubbles of air trapped in the ice sheets of Antarctica and Greenland. We can measure the gases in these bubbles if we extract a core of ice from Antarctica and take it back to a lab and melt it down inside a chamber. The other measurements we have of past atmospheric \(\text{CO}_2\) were made using scientific equipment starting with the work of David Keeling in 1957 that we read about in Chapter 1.
